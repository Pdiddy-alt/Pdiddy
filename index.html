Revealing the Formula

Exploring Statistical Trends in STE Entrance Exam Pass Rates

Abstract

This research investigates the underlying factors influencing success in the Science, Technology, and Engineering (STE) entrance examinations...

Introduction

The STE curriculum offers specialized learning tracks for high-achieving students in science and technology...

Review of Related Literature

  • York et al. (2015): GPA correlates with test performance.
  • Sirin (2005): Socioeconomic background affects academics.
  • Duckworth et al. (2007): Grit matters in test success.
  • Powers & Rock (1999): Review classes help.
  • Alcantara & Dizon (2020): Early exposure helps readiness.

Methodology

Sample: 300 applicants from 3 public schools (2021–2024)

Data: Grades, income, study time, reviews, exam results

Tools: Regression, Chi-square, Pearson (SPSS)

Results

  • GPA >90% → 68% pass rate
  • Study time strongly correlated (r = 0.61)
  • Review classes improved pass rate by 25%
  • High income = 30% more likely to pass
  • Females slightly outperformed males in science

Discussion

STE exam success is shaped by readiness, preparation, and support. It's not just intelligence — persistence and resources matter too.

Conclusion and Recommendations

  • Support low-income students
  • Start STEM early
  • Provide affordable review programs

Future Research: Expand regionally; explore long-term effects and psychology.
