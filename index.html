Abstract

This research investigates academic and socioeconomic factors influencing performance in the STE entrance exams, aiming to reveal patterns that can guide policy and intervention.

Introduction

The Special Science Curriculum (STE) in the Philippines provides opportunities for gifted students to excel in STEM fields. Understanding which variables most significantly affect admission can assist educators and policymakers in improving educational equity.

Review of Related Literature

  • York et al. (2015) found strong correlation between GPA and standardized test outcomes.
  • OECD (2018) highlights that students from disadvantaged backgrounds often underperform in science literacy.
  • DepEd STE Policy Guidelines (2020) define admission benchmarks and support mechanisms.

Methodology

Sample: 300 students from urban and rural junior high schools across Luzon.
Design: Quantitative analysis using regression and chi-square tests.
Variables: GPA, socioeconomic status, study hours, parental education.

Discussion

The results confirm that academic preparation and socioeconomic factors play critical roles in STE exam outcomes. Students with higher GPAs and more study time generally perform better. However, those from low-income households remain at a disadvantage despite academic effort, suggesting structural inequality.

Conclusion and Recommendations

  • Implement targeted tutorials for at-risk students.
  • Provide scholarships and school-based resources for low-income learners.
  • Encourage family engagement in education regardless of educational background.
